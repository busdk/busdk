# Bus Books Marketing Brief

## Working Product Definition

Bus Books is BusDK's product line for auditable bookkeeping on open workspace
data. It gives founders, bookkeepers, accountants, finance operators, and
technical evaluators a local bookkeeping UI backed by deterministic accounting
modules, attachments, reconciliation, validation, reports, formulas, document
rendering, CLI access, and API access over the same workspace data.

The current market focus should be practical bookkeeping, evidence, and
auditability. Bus Books may later support broader finance workflows, but the
public offer should stay anchored in books that users can inspect, validate,
reproduce, and export.

## Short Positioning

We help founders, bookkeepers, accountants, and finance operators keep
auditable books by offering local bookkeeping software built on deterministic
open workspace data, unlike opaque SaaS accounting tools, scattered
spreadsheets, or disconnected accountant portals, because Bus Books connects a
human-friendly UI, accounting engine, attachments, bank and reconciliation
workflows, VAT/reporting, validation, formulas, PDFs, CLI, and API over the
same evidence-backed data.

## Core Questions

### Goal

The immediate goal is qualified trust and serious evaluation, not broad hype.
The product page should convert visitors into one of these outcomes:

- Try a sample bookkeeping workspace.
- Import or inspect example accounting data.
- Review the audit/evidence model.
- Book a pilot or technical demo.
- Read the accounting engine, CLI, or API documentation.

Secondary goals are awareness among small business founders, bookkeepers,
accountants, auditors, and technical operators who want more control over
their finance data.

### Audience

Primary audience:

- Small business founders who want understandable books and less lock-in.
- Bookkeepers and accountants who need repeatable workflows and evidence.
- Finance operators who handle bank statements, invoices, attachments,
  reconciliation, VAT, periods, and reports.
- Technical founders or automation-minded operators who want CLI/API access to
  accounting data.

Technical evaluators and auditors are an important secondary audience. They
should be able to understand how the data is stored, how calculations are
produced, and how evidence connects to accounting entries.

### Problem

Small business bookkeeping often becomes hard to trust and hard to operate:

- Accounting data is scattered across SaaS tools, spreadsheets, PDFs, email
  attachments, bank exports, and accountant portals.
- Users cannot easily see the evidence behind a number.
- Reconciliation, VAT, periods, validation, and reporting become disconnected
  manual workflows.
- Automation is difficult when the UI, exports, and internal data model do not
  line up.
- Teams fear lock-in because the system of record is not plain, local, or easy
  to inspect.
- Accountants and founders may both see partial views of the same books.

### Offer

Bus Books offers auditable bookkeeping software built around one workspace
data model:

- Local bookkeeping web UI.
- Dashboard, inbox, journal, ledger/daybook, periods, VAT, bank, reconcile,
  reports, and validation workflows.
- Customer-facing accounting portal workflows through `bus-portal-accounting`.
- Attachments, files, evidence packages, and artifact preview/download flows.
- Data workbench surfaces for inspecting and shaping workspace data.
- Bus Formula Language for deterministic formulas and workbook extraction.
- PDF/document rendering for invoices, reports, and evidence packs.
- CLI and API access for automation and technical evaluation.
- Optional agent assistance where useful, without making AI the core trust
  claim.

### Value

The buyer should care because Bus Books makes accounting work more inspectable
and less locked into one interface:

- Use the UI, CLI, or API over the same workspace data.
- Keep evidence connected to the entries and reports it supports.
- Reproduce calculations through deterministic accounting modules and formulas.
- Reduce manual handoff between founder, bookkeeper, accountant, and auditor.
- Validate accounting data before periods, VAT, reports, or exports are relied
  on.
- Build automation without bypassing the human-facing bookkeeping workflow.

### Differentiation

Bus Books should be positioned against three alternatives:

- SaaS accounting tools: convenient, but often opaque, export-limited, and
  difficult to inspect deeply.
- Spreadsheets: flexible, but easy to break and weak as an auditable system of
  record.
- Disconnected accountant portals: useful for file exchange, but often
  separate from the actual accounting engine and automation surface.

BusDK's differentiators:

- Open workspace data is the center of the product.
- UI, CLI, and API operate over the same data instead of separate copies.
- The accounting engine is deterministic and explainable.
- Attachments, files, validation, reconciliation, reports, formulas, and PDF
  outputs are part of the same product line.
- The product can serve human bookkeeping workflows and technical automation
  workflows without hiding the source data.

### Proof

Avoid unsupported compliance or accuracy claims until the product has public
evidence. The marketing page should lead with demos and artifacts that BusDK
can actually show:

- A sample company workspace with imported invoices, receipts, bank data, and
  attachments.
- A workflow from inbox item to journal entry to reconciled bank transaction.
- A VAT or period report generated from inspectable source entries.
- A validation report showing detected issues and clean state.
- An evidence pack or PDF report that links numbers back to source documents.
- CLI/API examples reading the same workspace data shown in the UI.

Evidence to collect later:

- Time from bank statement import to first reconciled period.
- Number of validation issues caught before reporting.
- Export/import round-trip examples.
- Sample audit trail from source attachment to report line.
- Accountant or founder pilot feedback.

### Objections

Likely objections and how the page should answer them:

- "Will my accountant accept it?"
  Show exports, reports, evidence packs, and the deterministic accounting model.
- "Is it compliant?"
  Be precise. Do not imply universal compliance. Explain supported workflows,
  jurisdictions, validation, evidence, and what still needs professional
  review.
- "Can I get my data out?"
  Lead with open workspace data, CLI, API, and export-oriented workflows.
- "Is this safer than my current accounting SaaS?"
  Frame safety around inspectability, backups, evidence, and reproducible
  calculations, not vague security claims.
- "Is AI changing my books?"
  Make clear that optional agents can help with workflow, but the trust layer
  is deterministic data, validation, evidence, and human review.
- "Is it ready for my business?"
  Offer sample workspaces, pilots, supported workflow lists, and honest
  readiness notes.

### Tone

The tone should be calm, precise, trustworthy, and slightly premium. Accounting
buyers need confidence more than excitement.

Use:

- auditable bookkeeping
- open workspace data
- deterministic accounting engine
- evidence-backed entries
- validation
- reconciliation
- UI, CLI, and API over the same data
- inspectable reports
- local control

Avoid:

- magic accounting
- fully automated compliance
- effortless tax filing
- AI accountant
- replace your bookkeeper
- guaranteed correctness
- vague "real-time finance brain" claims
